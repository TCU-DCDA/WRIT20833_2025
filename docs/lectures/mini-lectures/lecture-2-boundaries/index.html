What is Taboo?

Taboo (from Polynesian tapu/kapu)

Cultural or religious boundaries around the sacred and dangerous

Violations bring sanctions - supernatural punishment, social ostracism

Traditional Example:

In some Polynesian cultures, a chief's shadow was considered so sacred that if anyone stepped on it, both the violator and the chief could face spiritual contamination or death.

Today's "sacred": Personal data and individual autonomy
