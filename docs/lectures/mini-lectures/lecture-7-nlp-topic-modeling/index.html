What is NLP?

Natural Language Processing

NLP is the field of computer science focused on enabling machines to understand, interpret, and generate human language.

You Use NLP Every Day:

  • Autocorrect and predictive text
  • Voice assistants (Siri, Alexa)
  • Spam filters
  • Translation apps
  • Search engines
  • Sentiment analysis of reviews

Why Language is Hard

Ambiguity

"I saw her duck"

Did you see her pet? Or her motion?

Context

"That's sick!"

Negative or positive? Depends on context.

Idioms

"It's raining cats and dogs"

Not literal. Cultural knowledge required.

Sarcasm

"Oh great, another meeting"

Words say positive, meaning is negative.

Key Challenge: Human language is messy, context-dependent, and culturally embedded

Deep Dive: Topic Modeling

The Research Question

"What are people talking about in this collection of 10,000 documents?"

The Human Problem:

Reading 10,000 documents would take months

Our cognitive biases might miss patterns

We need a bird's-eye view of themes

The Computational Solution:

Topic modeling automatically discovers hidden thematic structure in large text collections

7 / 12

Topic Modeling Explained

Definition

Topic modeling is an unsupervised machine learning technique that discovers abstract "topics" in a collection of documents.

Think of it like:

Imagine organizing a library without reading every book. You look at which words appear together frequently and infer categories: "history," "science fiction," "romance," etc.

Key Concepts:

  • Topics = Groups of words that frequently appear together
  • Documents = Mix of multiple topics
  • Algorithm = Finds patterns in word co-occurrence
8 / 12

LDA: The Most Common Algorithm

Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA)

LDA's Assumptions:

  1. Every document is a mixture of topics
  2. Every topic is a mixture of words
  3. Words that appear together likely share a topic

Simplified Process:

Step 1: Start with random topic assignments
Step 2: Iterate: "Which topic does this word probably belong to given its context?"
Step 3: Repeat thousands of times until patterns stabilize

Analogy:

Like reverse-engineering a recipe by analyzing finished dishes. You see patterns: tomatoes + basil + mozzarella = Italian cuisine topic

Critical Questions

Classification Logic at Work

What gets counted?

Topic models only work with text that's been digitized. Whose voices are in the archive? Whose are missing?

Who interprets?

Algorithms produce word clusters. Humans decide what they mean. Your cultural position shapes interpretation.

What assumptions are baked in?

LDA assumes topics are stable categories. But culture is fluid and contextual. Are we imposing order on chaos?

What does the algorithm miss?

Irony, sarcasm, coded language, cultural context. Computational reading has blind spots.
