When Coding Meets Culture

Developing Data-Driven Opinions through Computational Analysis

Context: This project builds on your approved HW5 proposal and the complete text analysis workflow you developed across HW4-1 and HW4-2. HTML/CSS training will prepare you for the digital essay component.

Project Overview

For your final project (groups of up to 3 members or individual work), you will execute the digital research project outlined in your HW5 proposal. Your project explores what happens when coding meets culture, providing a test case for your answer to this question:

"What insights emerge when we integrate term frequency analysis, sentiment analysis, and topic modeling with traditional humanities interpretation methods?"

What do computational and humanities approaches reveal together that neither could discover alone? How do you move from initial assumptions to well-grounded, data-driven opinions?

Project Requirements

Research Question & Methods

Building on your HW5 proposal, execute a research question that could not be answered using traditional humanities methods alone. Use the three base methods from HW4-1 and HW4-2:

  • Term Frequency
  • Sentiment Analysis (VADER)
  • Topic Modeling (Gensim)

Technical Standards

  • All code must be well-documented with clear comments
  • Cite all tutorials you use and any code you take from elsewhere
  • Notebooks should run cleanly and reproduce your results
  • Digital essay must demonstrate functional HTML/CSS skills (see Editing Digital Essay Guide)

Assessment Components

Written Content Structure

Overview (100-300 words)

Brief high-level overview of the project and how it builds on your HW4-1/HW4-2 work.

Research Question (200-300 words)

The question itself, background context, and how the question evolved from your proposal.

Methods (400-800 words)

Why you selected your methods, limitations addressed, data collection process, and reflection on data quality.

Findings (300-500 words)

What did your analysis reveal? How did results compare to your hypothesis? What limitations remain?

Integration & Reflection (200-300 words)

How did you integrate humanities and data? What did computation reveal that close reading could not?

Assessment Philosophy

This project prioritizes earned insight over clean code. You'll be evaluated on:

  • Depth of reflection and willingness to be surprised by your data
  • Evolution of thinking from initial predictions through final, data-driven opinions
  • Critical assessment of both your results and your analytical tools
  • Integration of technical analysis with humanistic interpretation

Technical execution matters, but intellectual growth and genuine insight are the primary goals.

Remember: Being "wrong" in your initial assumptions is not failure—it's evidence of genuine learning. The goal is not to confirm what you already thought, but to discover what emerges when computational analysis challenges cultural interpretation.
